The Task

In this study, we will test your ability to recall words based on different prompts across a series of trials.


On each trial, you will see a word or phrase prompt indicating a category.


As soon as you see the prompt, you should start verbally naming words that belong to the category.


You can decide, at any time, to move on to the next trial to recall words from a new category; once left, you cannot return to a category.


Your voice will be recorded, therefore, please speak clearly so that all the words you say are recorded.

                       

Scoring Rules


You will receive a score of one point for each valid recall for a category. If a recall isn't valid, you won't receive a point.


To ensure the recalled word is valid, please follow the following rules:

  1. Say words you think belong to a category, not just words that are related. (e.g, for category "TREES" valid recalls are "Sequoia" or "apple tree" but not "Leaves" or "Trunk")
  2. DO NOT repeat words within a category
  3. DO NOT say variations of the same word (e.g., for category "FOODS", "apple" and "apples" will not be considered as two separate valid recalls).

                       

Game Goal & Structure

Your main goal is to recall as many words as you can across the entire experiment.

You will do this task in six trial blocks with a break in-between. Each block will be about 5 minutes long.


The more words you recall, the higher your final compensation will be. Therefore, you should focus on maximizing your score within each block.
You will also receive a small bonus for covering more categories.


At any time, you can switch to a new category by pressing ENTER. If you feel you've run out of words, or are stuck, you should consider switching categories.
However, each time you change categories, you will have to wait before the new category label appears.
As such, there is a time penalty whenever you switch to a new category.
